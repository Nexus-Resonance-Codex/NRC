	Specifications for the Nexus Resonance Codex (NRC) include properties of $\varphi$-weighted Dirichlet-type series involving the golden ratio $\phi = (1 + \sqrt{5})/2$, modular residue stability under the Trageser Transformation Theorem (TTT), and high-dimensional lattice projections.

	\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, label=\textcolor{accentorange}{\textbullet}]
		\item $\phi$-weighted Dirichlet-type series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty \chi(n) \phi^{-kn} n^{-s}$ ($k \ge 1$) are characterized as entire functions with numerical zeros clustering near the line $\operatorname{Re}(s) \approx -\ln\phi \approx -0.48121$.
		\item The Trageser Transformation Theorem (TTT) establishes a modular residue stability principle excluding residues congruent to $\{0, 3, 6\} \pmod 9$.
		\item Functional transforms include the Trageser Transformation Theorem (TTT), Golden Tensor Theory (GTT), Multi-Scale Tensor Transform (MST), Trageser Universal Pattern Theorem (TUPT), and Quantum Residue Transform (QRT).
		\item High-dimensional lattice projections (256D--4096D) exhibit entropy scaling $H_d \approx 10.96 - \ln\phi \cdot \log d$.
	\end{itemize}
	
	Convergence results for the Trageser Universal Pattern Theorem (TUPT) and the Quantum Residue Turbulence (QRT) damping function are reported with empirical error bounds. Numerical verification utilizes variable-precision arithmetic ($dps \ge 80$). Applications are indicated in sparse representations, post-quantum signatures, and lattice-based cryptographic protocols.
